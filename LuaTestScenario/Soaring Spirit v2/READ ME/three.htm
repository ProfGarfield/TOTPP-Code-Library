Original concepts

I have used the stats of Aeterna Civitas II as a basis for this scenario's units. Although I was fairly happy with the stat system in Aeterna I found they favored the defender too much. I like the idea that the best form of defence is attack and have increased the offence of most unit types in an attemppt to create a more agressive game.

I decided to work out the units stats using the categorization system used in the De Bellis Antiquitatis (DBA) wargame rules. They divide units into groups defined by their role rather than simply their weapons or other criteria. This made testing and perfecting the stats much easier. The main types are:

KNIGHTS - Such as Persian Cavalry.

CAVALRY - Such as Thessalians and Tarentines.

LIGHT HORSE - Such as Numidians, Skythians and Horsemen.

SPEARS - Such as Hoplites and Spearmen.

PIKES - Such as Hellenic Hoplites.

BLADES - Such as Persian Takhabara.

AUXILIA - Such as Samnite Warriors.

BOWS - Such as Skythian Foot, Cretan Archers and Toxotoi.

PSILOI - Such as Psiloi and Rhodian Slingers

WARBAND - Such as Celts and Iberians.

ARTILLERY - Such as Battering Rams, Siege Towers and Catapults.

UNITS WORTHY OF A SPECIAL MENTION

 

Colonist 

The Colonist unit is the only one in the scenario allowed to build new cities. This must be done on a Colony Site square. They are expensive to build. This represents the substantial cost and preperation such an undertaking required.

 

Citizen

The citizen unit has been included to represent citizens joining a colony to boost its size. They can work the land too if required. These units will be useful in allowing far flung colonies to reach size six and send out their own Colonists.

 

Recruiter 

The Recruiter is allowed to bribe barbarian units and non Persian/Lydian units belonging to the Lydian faction. The recruiter also acts as a spy. The recruiter cannot steal technology or incite revolts.

 

Strategos 

The Strategos, or General, unit is generated randomly via events. It is the most powerful unit in the game and will probably spearhead attacks. As there is no telling how often these leaders will appear it is important not to throw them away recklessly.

 

Rich Village 

A Rich Village unit is placed on all unoccuppied Colony Site squares. Destroying these Rich Villages will yield an amount of gold and create a Slave unit in the attacking nations capital city.

 

Fortress 

Fortresses are strong defensive units pre placed in capitals and Minor cities. They will not be overcome easily so prepare your assaults carefully.

 

Eastern Fortress 

 

The Eastern Fortress has been used to defend the core Lydian cities. They are virtually invincible so don't even bother trying to kill them. I have included them to ensure the Lydians survive long enough for the Persian Invasion events to begin later in the game.
